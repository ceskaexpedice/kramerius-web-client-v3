The document is not publicly accessible

The document you requested may not be freely viewed in accordance with the Czech Copyright Law (no. 121/2000 Col.) and is only accessible onsite in the premises of the library owning the printed copy, on dedicated computers (terminals).

The Copyright Law allows for online access to a work 70 years after the death of the author (including translator, illustrator, etc.) and 50 years after the publication, beginning on 1st January of the year following the respective event. Unless the copyright in a work has expired, or the use thereof is governed by a special licences, a document included in the National Digital Library may not be freely viewed.

I can't get to the library, what should I do?

You can go to your nearest library and if they do not have a physical copy of the document, ask for it by way of the so-called interlibrary loan service. To obtain information about the availability of the document and the library that owns it, you can use the website of the central library portal Knihovny.cz.
